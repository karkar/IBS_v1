2.2 Testing Lactose / Dairy Consumption

On randomly pre-assinged days you will be asked to eat at least 8 grams of lactose during your first meal, ideally breakfast. It is ok to eat more than 8 grams on these days.

On other pre-assigned days you will be asked not to eat lactose during your first meal. You can eat lactose after logging your symptoms 4 hours after the meal.

Some examples of 8 grams of lactose are:

  • A cup of cottage cheese (225 grams)
  • A glass of cow's milk (8 oz)
  • A bowl of yogurt (5 oz)
